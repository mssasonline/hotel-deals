RECITALS

Whereas the First Party operates the SelectedRoom platform for luxury hotel bookings, operating under two models: last-minute bookings with time-based tiered pricing, and exclusive deals with fixed discounts offered by partner hotels. The Second Party wishes to join as a certified hotel partner to list its units on the Platform and attract bookings; the Parties have agreed to the following terms:

1

Definitions

The following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them below, unless the context requires otherwise:

Platform
The website and mobile application of SelectedRoom and all their associated services.
Partner
The Second Party signatory to this Agreement as the owner or duly authorised representative of the hotel property.
Hotel Unit
Rooms, suites, or residential units designated for listing on the Platform.
Confirmed Booking
Any booking successfully completed via the Platform with a confirmation number issued and payment processed.
Commission
The agreed percentage due to the First Party from the total value of each Confirmed Booking.
Net Rate
The amount due to the Partner after deducting the Platform's commission and agreed processing fees.
Confidential Information
Any commercial, financial, or technical data exchanged between the Parties in the course of performing this Agreement.
Guest
The end user who made a booking via the Platform and will stay at the Partner's hotel unit.
2

Subject Matter and Scope

2.1  This Agreement grants the Second Party the status of "Certified Hotel Partner" to list the hotel units specified in Appendix A on the SelectedRoom platform, subject to the terms set out herein.

2.2  The scope of the partnership includes: listing hotel units, managing rates and availability, receiving bookings, collecting payments from Guests, and transferring dues to the Partner.

2.3  This Agreement does not create any employment relationship, legal agency, or commercial partnership in the commercial sense. Each Party remains independently responsible for its own legal and tax obligations.

2.4  Full sovereignty over the Platform, booking mechanisms, and user experience remains exclusively with the First Party.

4

Partner Obligations

4.1 Data Accuracy:  The Partner shall provide the Platform with accurate and complete information for all hotel units, including: description, current photographs, facilities, pricing, and cancellation policies. The Partner bears full responsibility for any errors in this data.

4.2 Availability:  The Partner shall update availability in real time and shall not sell units booked via the Platform through other channels (double booking). Should this occur, the Partner shall bear the full cost of accommodating the Guest at an equivalent or superior alternative property.

4.3 Service Quality:  The Partner shall deliver service consistent with the declared star rating and category of the hotel property, and shall respond to Guest complaints within (24) hours of receipt.

4.4 Licensing and Compliance:  The Partner warrants that it holds all required government licences and approvals to operate the hotel property, and shall comply with all applicable local laws and regulations.

4.5 Rate Parity:  The Partner shall maintain rate parity, ensuring that prices listed on the Platform are no higher than those available through the hotel's direct channels.

4.6 Insurance:  The Partner shall maintain valid insurance coverage including third-party liability throughout the term of this Agreement.

5

Platform Obligations

5.1 Listing and Promotion:  The First Party shall list the Partner's hotel units on the Platform and present them professionally to the targeted guest audience.

5.2 Booking Management:  The First Party shall manage the entire booking process, including booking confirmation and notification to both the Partner and the Guest.

5.3 Payment Collection and Taxes:  Acting as an agent, the First Party shall collect the full amount from the Guest (room value + breakfast + government taxes), then remit to the Partner: their net share as per the commission rate agreed in Article 6, plus all collected government taxes (Municipality fees, Tourism Dirham, VAT 5%). The Partner shall bear responsibility for remitting these taxes to the competent government authorities. The First Party shall pay VAT only on its own commission income (5% of the commission amount due).

5.4 Technical Support:  The First Party shall provide the Partner with electronic management tools (dashboard) to monitor bookings, revenue, and performance, along with technical support during business hours.

5.5 Partner Data Confidentiality:  The First Party shall not disclose the Partner's commercial or financial data to third parties without prior written consent, except as required by regulatory authorities.

Note: The commission rate is determined by mutual agreement between the Parties and recorded in the box above at the time of signing. This rate may vary from one partner to another depending on the scope of the partnership and the nature of the offerings.

6.1 Calculation Basis:  Commission is calculated on the total booking value including service charges and breakfast, excluding government taxes (Municipality fees, Tourism Dirham, VAT).

6.2 Rate Review:  Either Party may request a review of the commission rate by providing (30) days' written notice. No amendment shall take effect without the written agreement of both Parties.

6.3 Monthly Settlement Schedule:  Partner dues are aggregated and settled on a monthly basis. The total of all bookings where both check-in and check-out occurred within the preceding calendar month shall be transferred to the Partner's bank account within (7) business days of the start of the following month. Bookings that began in the preceding month but where the Guest had not yet checked out shall be carried forward in full to the settlement of the month in which actual check-out occurs.

6.4 Currency:  All settlements shall be made in UAE Dirhams (AED) unless otherwise agreed in writing.

6.5 Revenue Reports:  The First Party shall provide the Partner with a detailed monthly statement of bookings, amounts transferred, and commissions charged.

6.6 Automatic Suspension:  If the Partner's booking cancellation rate exceeds (15%) over 30 consecutive days, the First Party reserves the right to suspend the account automatically until the causes are resolved.

7

Cancellation, No-Show, and Refund Policy

Platform Policy: All bookings completed via the Platform are immediate and non-cancellable or refundable once payment is confirmed. Guests explicitly agree to this policy prior to completing their booking.

7.1 Non-Cancellable Bookings:  All bookings made via the Platform are final and non-cancellable by the Guest once payment is completed. No refund shall be due under any circumstances, except as provided under Article 12 (Force Majeure) of this Agreement.

7.2 No-Show:  In the event a Guest fails to arrive without prior notice, the Partner shall retain the full booking value and no refund shall be issued. The First Party shall notify the Partner of the no-show as soon as it becomes aware.

7.3 Partner Inability to Accommodate:  Should the Partner be unable to accommodate the Guest due to internal circumstances (such as closure, maintenance, or double booking), the Partner shall: immediately notify the First Party, bear the full cost of accommodating the Guest at an alternative property of equivalent or superior standard, and compensate the Guest for any price difference or direct damages arising therefrom.

8

Intellectual Property and Usage Rights

8.1  All trademarks, logos, and visual content belonging to the Platform remain the exclusive property of the First Party. The Partner may not use them beyond the scope of promoting the partnership without prior written consent.

8.2  The Partner grants the First Party a non-exclusive licence to use the hotel's photographs, descriptions, and content for digital marketing purposes during the term of this Agreement.

8.3  Upon termination of this Agreement, each Party shall immediately cease using any rights granted by the other Party.

9

Confidentiality

9.1  Both Parties undertake to maintain the confidentiality of all commercial, financial, and technical information exchanged between them, and shall not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent from the other Party.

9.2  Exceptions include: information already in the public domain, or information required to be disclosed by a competent judicial or regulatory authority.

9.3  This obligation shall survive for three (3) years following the expiry or termination of this Agreement.

10

Data Protection

10.1  Both Parties shall process Guests' personal data in accordance with the applicable data protection laws in the United Arab Emirates and the regulations of the Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security.

10.2  The Partner shall not use the personal data of Guests booked via the Platform for its own marketing purposes without obtaining their explicit consent.

10.3  Each Party shall notify the other within (48) hours of discovering any security breach affecting shared Guest data.

11

Liability and Indemnification

11.1  The First Party shall not be liable to the Guest for the quality of hotel services or for any material or moral damage suffered by the Guest during their stay at the Partner's property.

11.2  The Partner shall indemnify the Platform against any claims or legal proceedings arising from its breach of the obligations set out in this Agreement.

11.3  The maximum liability of either Party shall in no event exceed the total commissions paid during the three (3) months preceding the incident in dispute.

11.4  Neither Party shall be liable for indirect, incidental damages or loss of anticipated profits.

12

Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be deemed in breach of its obligations if prevented from performing them by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, pandemics, government orders, wars, or civil unrest. The affected Party shall notify the other Party in writing within (5) business days of the commencement of such event, and the affected obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the force majeure. If the event continues for more than (60) days, either Party may terminate this Agreement without penalty.

13

Termination

13.1 Termination with Notice:  Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing written notice (30) days prior to the proposed termination date, while honouring all existing bookings.

13.2 Immediate Termination:  The First Party may terminate this Agreement immediately without prior notice in the following cases: material breach of the Agreement's terms, provision of fraudulent information, insolvency, or any activity damaging to the Platform's reputation or contrary to applicable laws.

13.3 Effects of Termination:  Upon termination of this Agreement in any manner, all outstanding financial dues between the Parties shall be settled within (14) business days, and all future bookings shall be cancelled with amounts refunded to Guests.

14

Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

14.1 Amicable Settlement:  The Parties shall first seek to resolve any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement amicably within a period not exceeding (30) days from the date one Party notifies the other of the dispute.

14.2 Arbitration:  If amicable settlement is not achieved, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration under the rules of the Dubai International Arbitration Centre (DIAC) by a single arbitrator agreed upon by the Parties. The language of arbitration shall be English.

14.3 Governing Law:  This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the United Arab Emirates.

15

General Provisions

15.1 Entire Agreement:  This Agreement and its appendices constitute the entire and final agreement between the Parties and supersede all prior negotiations and agreements relating to the same subject matter.

15.2 Amendments:  No amendment to this Agreement shall be valid unless documented in writing and signed by both Parties.

15.3 Non-Assignment:  Neither Party may assign its rights or obligations under this Agreement to a third party without the prior written consent of the other Party.

15.4 Severability:  If any provision of this Agreement is found to be legally unenforceable, all remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

15.5 Notices:  All formal notices shall be in writing and sent to the other Party's registered email address or by registered post with acknowledgement of receipt.

15.6 Counterparts:  This Agreement has been executed in two original counterparts of equal standing, one retained by each Party.
